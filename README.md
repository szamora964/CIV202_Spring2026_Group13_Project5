## Project 5

Natural hazards can create serious impacts for communities across the United States. These hazards can damage property, disrupt transportation and infrastructure, affect local economies, and put people’s safety at risk. Because of this, Risk Averse, LLC has asked our group of engineers to continue analyzing natural disaster risk using data from the Federal Emergency Management Agency’s National Risk Index, also known as the NRI.

For this project, our team will focus on one specific natural hazard across six different states. The purpose of this analysis is to compare how the chosen hazard affects different areas and to identify regional trends in the data. By looking at states that are located close to each other, we can better understand how hazard risk changes across nearby communities. This project will use NRI Census Tract data, along with population-related information, to study both the level of risk and the number of people who may be affected by the hazard.

The NRI dataset includes important risk factors such as Expected Annual Loss, Social Vulnerability, and Community Resilience. These factors help explain not only where a hazard may cause the most damage, but also how prepared or vulnerable communities may be. However, risk can be defined in different ways depending on the purpose of the analysis. In this project, our team may use the original NRI definition of risk or create our own risk method using available NRI and SVI data. If a new risk method is used, the calculations will be clearly explained and justified.

To complete the analysis, our team will clean, organize, and compare the data for the selected hazard across the six states. We will create maps, tables, and graphs to show how hazard risk differs by location and how it relates to population. One major goal is to create a summary plot that highlights an important trend in the data and is easy for Risk Averse, LLC shareholders to understand. Another goal is to show how population is connected to hazard risk, since a hazard in a highly populated area may have a larger impact on people and communities.

Overall, this project will help Risk Averse, LLC better understand the risk of one natural hazard across multiple states. The final analysis will show where risk is highest, how the selected states compare, and how population should be considered when measuring natural hazard impacts. This work will help explain why risk analysis is important for planning, emergency preparation, and supporting communities that may be affected by natural disasters.
